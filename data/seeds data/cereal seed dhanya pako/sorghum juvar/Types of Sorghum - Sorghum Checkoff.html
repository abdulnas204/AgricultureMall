Types of Sorghum

Pearled sorghum grain is any sorghum that has been through a milling process to remove the pericarp. It can be used in place of rice and pasta and is excellent in salads.

Sorghum bran is a delicious, easy way to add fiber to your diet. Available in burgundy, black or sumac, sorghum bran is rich in antioxidants and has a high ORAC value.

White pearled grain sorghum flour is made from decorticated sorghum. Light in color and neutral in flavor, white sorghum flour works exceptionally well when baking pastries.

Whole grain sorghum flour is ground from tan/white sorghum. It is an ideal, gluten-free option for baked food products including breads, muffins, pizzas, pastas, cookies, cakes and more. It’s neutral, sometimes sweet, flavor and light color make it easily adaptable to a variety of dishes.

Sorghum syrup has been used as a sweetener in the U.S. since the 1850’s. It is a great addition to your breakfast table.

Popped sorghum looks like miniature kernels of popped corn without the hull. It’s a simple snack the kids will love.

Flaked sorghum makes an excellent addition to breakfast cereals, energy bars, cookies and granola snacks.
